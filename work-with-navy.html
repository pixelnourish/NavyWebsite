What's it like working with Navy?
Our beliefs about design have a big effect on how we collaborate with our clients. Here are the five simple principles that govern the way we work.

We believe good design comes from

Open minded research

We think the best way to understand a design problem is unbiased face to face contact with the people involved. This means you'll learn interesting things about how your audience thinks and behaves.

"Navy's research led approach to design helped us develop a truly innovative strategy for a loyalty programme with over 10 million members."

Mike Efron

Digital Experience Director at Coles

Coles

We believe good design comes from

Testing prototypes and evolving them over time

We learn by making mistakes in the real world rather than aiming for perfection in the abstract.
This means you'll see the design evolve as we learn about how people use it.

"Navy's user testing and recommendations really showed us where the product should be going in the future."

Martin Freeman

Practice Manager at Telstra

Telstra

We believe good design comes from

Attention to aesthetics

We think good design is beautiful as well as functional.
This means your team and your audience will develop a real emotional connection to the product.

"Navy's visual design added a new level of sophistication to our product. This premium aesthetic helped us position our product for the enterprise market."

Romilly Blackburn

Chief Operating Officer at Whispir

Whispir

We believe good design comes from

Working closely with developers

We think collaboration between designers and engineers is at the heart of great technology.
This means we work with everyone from enterprise level teams to agile startups.

Our design team:

  • Is experienced integrating with agile or waterfall
  • Is open to co-location
  • Prototypes to document high level functionality
  • Works with BAs to provide the next level of detail
  • Can deliver pixel perfect HTML if required

We believe good design comes from

Open, flexible collaboration with stakeholders

We embrace changing requirements and needs. We define success by real outcomes for people, not how closely we adhere to a checklist of features. This means we consider it our responsibility to manage the involvement of project

"Our project had over 30 different stakeholders. Navy's work unified them behind a single vision for our new product."

Paul Strike
