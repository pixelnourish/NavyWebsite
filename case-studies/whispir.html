Whispir is web based software for sending messages in an emergency.

It's used by some of the biggest emergency services organisations in the Asia Pacific region, including the Australian police, firefighters and ambulance workers.

We worked with the Whispir product team to design the interfaces for the web based application, mobile apps and mobile site.

Project summary

 
Audience Firefighters, police and ambulance
 
Technology Web based software & mobile apps

What we did

We worked closely with Whispir's product team on detailed user experience design.

The result

A brand new interface for the product, live in time for fire season.

Real world example: How firefighters at the CFA use Whispir

Black Saturday taught the emergency services in Victoria some hard lessons about communication.

"Communications systems on Black Saturday were hindered by poor coverage, lack of interoperability between emergency services agencies, and insufficient investment in new technologies."

— The 2009 Victorian Bushfires Royal Commission

On the recommendations of the Victorian Bushfires Royal Commission, the CFA started using the Whispir platform to co-ordinate their response to bushfires.

From the team

Brett on how research with the CFA informed our design work

1 min 20 secs

Here's how reliable messaging helps the community in country Victoria.

  • Social media

    Important updates are simultaneously published on social media channels like the CFA's Twitter feed.

    When information is this important, using multiple channels is critical.

  • Mission control at the CFA

    The CFA's mission control can monitor all the information about a bushfire and send updates to anyone who needs to know.

  • Firefighters in the field

    Firefighters in the field can use Whispir's mobile apps to recieve instructions and send updates on the situation to other teams.

  • Mainstream media outlets

    The mainstream media recieve live updates which they can immediately pass on to the public.

  • People who live in affected areas

    People in affected areas recieve updates via voice, SMS and email. They can also provide information from the ground back to the CFA.

In critical situations, mistakes can be costly.
We worked hard to create an interface that's fast and easy to use.
