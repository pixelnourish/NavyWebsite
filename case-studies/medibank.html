Healthbook is part of the Australian Government's focus on eHealth services

Medibank won a multi-million dollar government contract to create a pilot version of the government's eHealth record. We worked with Medibank to design the Healthbook system.

Project summary

Technology Web based software

What we did

We created a strategic vision for Healthbook, and then completed detailed user experience design of every screen.

Medibank - For Better Health Australian Government

Here’s how Healthbook works

 

In Australia, people's health records are stored at their individual providers.

For example, heart test results are stored with the cardiologist and prescriptions are stored at the pharmacy.

Healthbook was created to bring all of those medical records together in one place.

The goal is to make sure the right people have the right information at the right time.

To test the Healthbook concept we designed a pilot version for a particularly challenging audience.

 

The average age of participants in the pilot was 73 years old.

They suffered from a variety of chronic health conditions

How do elderly people with chronic illness think about their health?

William*, 73 on seeing his doctors:

How do people mentally categorise their health information? By condition? By date?

Our research made it clear that people think of their health in terms of interactions with people. Elderly people have typically seen the same doctors for many years, and refer to them primarily by name. We grouped lists of appointments and records by medical professional, and let people customise their descriptions.

eHealth doesn't mean the end of paper.

Many people we spoke to carried important medical information with them "just in case."

This blue card is a record of someone's heart valve transplant.

To make sure these people had an up to date record, we designed a printable summary of their Healthbook which could fit on a single A4 page.

Our research meant we were able to make some simple recommendations on which features to leave out.
